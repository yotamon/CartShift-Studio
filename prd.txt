CartShift Studio Website – Product Requirements Document
Project Overview

CartShift Studio is a bold and creative e-commerce development agency specializing in Shopify and WordPress solutions. The website will serve as the digital face of CartShift Studio, reflecting its colorful, playful yet elegant and professional brand personality. As a boutique agency co-led by a Technical Lead (the user) and a Customer-Facing Partner (the user’s friend), CartShift Studio’s site must convey expertise, trustworthiness, and approachability. The primary purpose of the website is to generate leads and showcase CartShift’s core services in Shopify and WordPress development. It will combine engaging design with strategic content to convert a broad range of potential clients – product creators, artists, entrepreneurs, and businesses across industries – into inquiries and long-term clients.

Target Audience

The website will communicate to a broad range of potential clients who want to sell or grow online. This includes:

Product Creators & Artists: Individuals or small teams (e.g. artisans, designers, content creators) launching their own products or art online, who need an easy-to-manage store with a strong brand presence.

Entrepreneurs & Startups: New business owners or startups across any industry (fashion, tech gadgets, wellness, etc.) seeking expert help to build their e-commerce website from scratch or scale an existing one.

Established Businesses & Brands: Companies (retailers, manufacturers, etc.) looking to expand into e-commerce or improve their current online store’s performance (e.g. migrating from WooCommerce to Shopify, enhancing site speed, implementing custom features).

Agencies or Partners: (Secondary audience) Other agencies or designers who might seek CartShift’s technical expertise in Shopify/WordPress for their projects (this could be noted, but primary focus is end-clients).

User Needs & Mindset: These audiences may not be tech experts – they seek a partner who can simplify the complexity of e-commerce development. They value creativity (to make their brand stand out) and professionalism (to trust with their business). The site’s content should therefore educate and reassure them: showing understanding of common pain points (e.g. “How do I set up a Shopify store that converts?”) and offering CartShift as the solution provider. Messaging will focus on how CartShift Studio helps clients achieve their goals (more sales, better UX, scalable online presence) rather than just listing technical skills.

Not all competitors highlight ongoing support; some frame their work as project-based. By underlining our long-term support commitment, we attract clients who worry about post-launch. This also plays to customers who had bad experiences with freelancers or agencies that disappeared after delivery.

Portfolio: If competitors have an extensive portfolio, they might impress with big names. If CartShift is newer without big brand logos yet, our approach is to emphasize quality and personal commitment over quantity. We can include even small project stories but frame them as impactful (e.g., how we helped a small artist go from zero to an online business – that narrative can be as powerful as saying we worked with a Fortune 500, just targeted to a different audience). In time, as our portfolio grows, we will integrate it. In the interim, our blog can serve as pseudo-portfolio (case study styled posts about what can be done).

Some competitor sites are not updated frequently (outdated blogs or copyright year). We will keep our content fresh (update blog, maybe show a “recent post date”) and ensure the site always looks maintained (this subtly signals an active, thriving agency).

The emotional appeal (colors, approachable language) can give us an edge in likeability and first impression.
